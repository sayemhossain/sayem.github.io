Best Overall: Bake Me a Wish

Sign Up Now

Founded by a guy who wanted to send a birthday cake to his mother, Bake Me A Wish earns the Best Overall rating for its large selection, easy ordering capabilities, and reasonable shipping costs. With selections like strawberry funfetti cake, golden fudge celebration cake, and rainbow cake, you’ll find the perfect flavor for whoever's celebrating.

If you’re throwing a big bash, Bake Me a Wish offers large cakes like the colossal red velvet birthday cake, which serves up to 24 people, as well as bakery towers that include cookies, cupcakes, brownies, and other goodies. Plus, every birthday cake comes in a colorful gift box along with a personalized birthday card.

Prices are competitive, and for extra savings, you can check out the company's daily deal, and sign up for their loyalty program to get rewards and discount codes. Bake Me A Wish is also certified kosher.

Best Budget: Instacart

Sign Up Now

Not every birthday cake needs to be iconic, shipped overnight, or cost you an arm and a leg. Sometimes the local market has just what you need and can help when you're too busy to leave home to get it.

Operating in over 5,000 North American cities, Instacart leverages technology to connect you with one of its "personal shoppers," who fulfill your order and deliver it to your door within hours. When compared with overnight shipping costs, Instacart’s delivery fees are minimal, and monthly and yearly memberships are available that include free delivery on orders over $35.

Instacart has partnered with national chains like Costco, Target, and Kroger, so birthday cake options include everything from Kirkland’s signature vanilla 1/2 sheet cake, serving 48 (Costco), to a box of eight Little Debbie birthday cakes (Target). Just put "cake" on your shopping list, and search different stores until you find what you want—or at least something close enough.

Best for Foodies: Goldbelly

Sign Up Now

Goldbelly’s curated online marketplace is a hub of North America’s most notable culinary creations. The company's tag line is "order iconic foods nationwide," and indeed, the marketplace features items like the pastrami sandwich from New York’s Carnegie Deli, St. Viateur’s bagels from Montreal, “Pie Holes” from Los Angeles, and a whole lot more. So, when you’re shopping for that iconic birthday cake for a foodie in your life, Goldbelly is the place to start. 

The site—there’s also a smartphone app—offers cakes from many of America’s leading bakeries like Milk Bar, Ovenly, Carlo’s, Gambino’s, Carousel, We Take the Cake, and Caroline’s. Many bakeries, such as Ovenly, Red Truck, and Magnolia sell online exclusively through Goldbelly. 

Goldbelly facilitates the ordering and shipping process, in many cases for one flat fee, although some items do require extra shipping charges. Layer cakes start around $50. Goldbelly’s selection is extensive with something for everyone, including vegan, gluten-free, kosher, and even paleo cakes.

The company ships to all 50 states. But shipping times vary, as many bakeries only ship on certain days of the week.

Most Festive: Milk Bar

Sign Up Now

Looking for a cake that delivers the ultimate birthday essence? Check out Milk Bar’s birthday cakes. There are only three choices: birthday cake, chocolate birthday cake, and strawberry shortcake—plus mini and gluten-free versions of the regular birthday cake flavor.

But what Milk Bar’s menu lacks in breadth it more than makes up for with depth of craft. The website touts the three years the Milk Bar team worked—led by founder and James Beard award-winning pastry chef Christina Tosi—to achieve the perfect nostalgic birthday funfetti flavor. With that kind of commitment and attention to detail, it’s no wonder that Milk Bar has garnered heaps of positive press and hundreds of enthusiastic customer reviews.

Prices start at $50 for the mini birthday cake. It's $15 for flat rate shipping. Milk Bar also offers birthday gift sets that include additional items like cake truffles, cookies, Tosi’s book "All About Cake," and Milk Bar pie. You can order ahead, and even select a specific delivery day up to 30 days in advance. Milk Bar is also available through Goldbelly.

Best for Chocolate Lovers: Carousel Cakes

Sign Up Now

If your birthday recipient loves chocolate, you may want to browse the goodies from Carousel Cakes of Nanuet, New York. Founded in 1965, Carousel is a trusted institution. The bakery's online menu offers around two dozen different layer cakes, nine of which are packed with chocolate, including chocolate mousse cake, New York blackout, and rocky road.

In addition to its chocolate specialties, Carousel also crafts quintessential birthday flavors like its nonpareil giggle cake and sprinkle happy cake. Fun fact: Carousel's blue and red velvet cakes have been featured on Oprah’s list of favorite things.

The prices at Carousel fall on the high-end. Carousel's chocolate outrage cake ends up costing over $100 with overnight shipping, the only shipping option offered.

Shipments are only Monday through Thursday, so plan ahead. All of Carousel’s cakes are certified kosher.

Best for Food Sensitivities: Caroline’s Cakes

Sign Up Now

Among the many reasons Caroline’s Cakes stands out is its commitment to operating a nut-free bakery. Caroline’s employs strict measures to ensure no nuts can sneak in to spoil your birthday celebration. The bakery also has gluten-free options, so you can avoid any issues on that front as well.

Caroline’s website offers a wide variety of cakes and other goodies, including 10 whimsical birthday cakes decorated with giant multi-colored polka dots. Flavors include their famous seven-layer caramel cake, rainbow cake, and four-layer chocolate devil’s food cake. If you can’t decide which flavor you want, check out the "half cakes" option, allowing you to combine two different flavors into one cake. Unfortunately, this option is not available in their signature birthday polka dot flavor. 

Each round, nine-inch cake serves between 14 and 20 people and starts at $60, plus shipping. There's also an option to add 16 polka dot plates, tableware, and candles that spell out "happy birthday," to your order for an extra fee.

Most Customizable: Send a Cake

Sign Up Now

Birthday cake doesn’t have to involve a big party. When you want to send all your birthday love to one special individual, look no further than the personalized cake explosion boxes from Send A Cake.

Choose a flavor from eight options, a box design, and up to seven additional decorations to create a customized experience your loved one will never forget. You can spell the recipient’s name on the lid in bejeweled letters, add wind-up butterflies that emerge from the box, have the inside of the box printed with your own photos, and more. The mini cakes are three-and-a-half inches in diameter and come in flavors such as loaded confetti sprinkle, chocolate tuxedo, and red velvet sparkle.

Send A Cake offers delivery six days a week, with free shipping on orders of $85 or more. They also accept orders well in advance. In fact, with Send A Cake, you could arrange your whole year of birthday gifting in one day.

Best Local: Postmates

Sign Up Now

Like Instacart, Postmates is a technology platform for local couriers. But instead of partnering with grocery stores, Postmates focuses on shops and restaurants.

While Postmates does deliver from grocery stores, its in-app selections from local businesses are much more limited than Instacart’s. In fact, Postmates really shines by featuring popular neighborhood establishments. Founder Bastian Lehmann has referred to his company as "the anti-Amazon," because while Amazon threatens local proprietors with crushing competition, Postmates helps mom-and-pop shops join the ever-growing, on-demand marketplace.

Postmates operates in thousands of cities in the U.S. Delivery times and fees vary, but in general are under two hours and $10, respectively. Depending on the options in your area, you could plan a birthday party in the morning, order in the afternoon, and receive the cake, all before the sun goes down.
